# Centrifugal Pump System – Design & Performance Analysis

## Overview
This project focuses on the design, assembly, and experimental performance evaluation of a centrifugal pump system. The pump was designed using SolidWorks and tested under varying head and flow conditions to evaluate its operational characteristics, including flow rate, head, and efficiency.

The pump operates by rotating an impeller to impart kinetic energy to the fluid, creating a pressure differential that enables fluid transfer.

## Project Objectives
- Design and assemble a centrifugal pump system
- Measure pump head, flow rate, and electrical power
- Calculate pump efficiency
- Analyze pump performance trends
- Validate experimental results against expected centrifugal pump behavior


## System Design
The pump assembly consists of:
- Pump inlet and outlet ports
- Impeller
- Pump body and faceplate
- Bronze bushing
- DC motor
- Fasteners and housing

SolidWorks was used to create both **assembly** and **exploded views** to verify component interaction, alignment, and manufacturability.

## Experimental Setup
Testing was conducted using:
- 12V DC power supply
- Multimeters for voltage and current measurement
- Water column to control pump head

- Valves for flow regulation
- Measuring cup and stopwatch for flow rate calculation
- Vinyl tubing and external water reservoir

images/pump_test_setup.png

Each trial was conducted over a **20-second interval** to ensure consistent and controlled testing conditions.

## Key Results
- **Maximum Pump Head:** 1.95 m  
- **Peak Flow Rate:** 1.24 L/min  
- **Maximum Efficiency:** 4.9%  
- **Peak Efficiency Head:** 1.76 m  
- **Typical Operating Voltage:** 12 V  
- **Typical Operating Current:** ~0.29 A  

## Performance Analysis

### Pump Head vs Flow Rate
The pump exhibits the expected inverse relationship characteristic of centrifugal pumps: as pump head increases, flow rate decreases.
<img width="505" height="400" alt="Head_v s_flow" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/78dbe1ef-1283-4454-a631-0247bc43183c" />


### Pump Efficiency vs Pump Head
Pump efficiency increases with head up to a peak point and then decreases beyond optimal conditions. Peak efficiency occurred near **1.76 m head**, matching centrifugal pump theory.

images/efficiency_vs_head.png

## Engineering Interpretation
- Electrical input remained nearly constant, indicating head variation does not significantly affect voltage or current in this configuration
- Flow rate increased as head decreased due to reduced flow resistance
- Efficiency peaked under moderate head conditions, highlighting optimal operating range
- Valve shutoff tightness significantly affected pump performance
